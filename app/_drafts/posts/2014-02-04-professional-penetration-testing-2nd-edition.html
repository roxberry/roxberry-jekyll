Professional Penetration Testing, 2nd Edition is an introduction to Penetration Testing as a career.  The book opens with “Ethics and Hacking”.  I appreciate that this was discussed early in the book.  It served to set the tone for acceptable behavior by potential pen testers.

Chapter 3 discusses setting up a virtual lab and an actual hardware lab.  The book then moves into more tactical information, Chapter 4 introduces frameworks and methodologies and their respective advantages and disadvantages.

Following Chapter 4, subsequent chapters discuss reconnaissance and exploitation, internal attacks and then briefly touches on web application attacks.  The last 2 chapters address Penetration Testing in a more formal manner and what the job of a penetration tester entails.  Chapter 14 discusses report creation and what resources are available to communicate metrics, techniques and mitigations.  Chapter 15 discusses how to Penetration Testing can be a long term profession, including certifications and training.
